# Foil Geometry Optimizer (NACA-based)

## Description
This project focuses on the generation and optimization of hydrofoil/aerofoil profiles. While many modern approaches rely on complex parametric representations (such as Kulfan curves), this tool utilizes a more pragmatic approach based on established NACA airfoils, enhanced with a custom "reflex" parameter for refined performance control.

## Problem Statement
Initial research into foil design using high-degree parametric curves often resulted in geometries that reached physical limitations, failing to meet required lift-to-drag ratios. To address these constraints, a robust alternative was needed to ensure both geometric feasibility and aerodynamic performance.

## Technical Approach
To overcome the limitations of the previous model, I implemented the following:
* **NACA Integration:** Leveraging standard NACA profiles as a reliable foundation to ensure aerodynamic stability.
* **Custom Reflex Control:** Developed a local deformation function at the trailing edge (reflex). This allows for precise pitch moment control and enhanced lift management without compromising structural integrity.
* **Integrated Drag Analysis:** To refine the realism of the simulation, I expanded the drag model beyond the wing geometry. By including the mast and fuselage in the aerodynamic calculations, I was able to achieve a more accurate prediction of the total moment and system drag, resulting in a design that better reflects real-world conditions.
* **Static Margin Management:** I initially struggled with the sensitivity of the static margin, that was extremely high (90%) for "freeride". Through iterative testing (and the addition of mast and fuselage), I obtained a lower theoretical static margin (~60%). This value emerged as a physical constraint necessary to maintain longitudinal trim (M=0) given the chosen fuselage length and aerodynamic center. I concluded that this represents a 'natural' stability inherent to the configuration, and deemed it as an acceptable trade-off to maintain ease of use without significantly compromising the overall design goals.
* **Empirical Validation:** The model was calibrated to converge toward known, proven foil geometries. By benchmarking the output against established designs, I ensured that the generated foils remain within realistic and efficient operating bounds.

## Optimization Methodology & Limitations
This script relies on numerical optimization (IPOPT). Due to the highly non-linear nature of hydrofoil aerodynamics:
* **Sensitivity:** The solver is highly sensitive to initial conditions. For specific design targets (e.g., pumping vs. high-speed windsurf), "initial guesses" might require fine-tuning to ensure convergence. As constraints seem very tight on the optimisation, the model requires fine calibration of the initial parameters to converge towards physically viable solutions, and often it doesn't find any.
* **Empirical Calibration:** The tool is designed as a design-aid rather than a fully automated generator. It requires an iterative approach to find the optimal balance between performance and structural constraints.
